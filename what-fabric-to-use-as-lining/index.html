What Fabric To Use As Lining A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Jun 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As a home sewer or professional tailor, one of the most important decisions you have to make is what fabric to use as lining. The lining is what gives a garment its structure, shape, and durability, and choosing the wrong fabric can result in a poorly fitting or uncomfortable piece of clothing.

Many people struggle with the decision of what fabric to use as lining, especially if they are new to sewing or tailoring. There are several pain points that come with this decision, including the weight and drape of the fabric, the color and texture, and the overall cost of the project.

The ideal fabric to use as lining depends on the type of garment you are making, as well as the look and feel you are going for. However, there are a few general guidelines that can help you make an informed decision.

Choosing a Lightweight Fabric

One of the most important factors to consider when choosing a lining fabric is its weight. You want a lightweight fabric that won't add bulk to your garment, but is still strong enough to provide structure and support. Some good options include silk, rayon, cotton voile, and lightweight polyester blends.

Personally, I like to use silk as a lining fabric for dresses and jackets. It has a beautiful drape and adds a luxurious feel to the garment. Rayon is another good option that is affordable and easy to work with, and cotton voile is perfect for summer dresses and blouses.

Consider the Color and Texture

The color and texture of your lining fabric can also make a big difference in the finished garment. If you are making a light-colored or sheer garment, you may want to choose a lining in a matching or complementary color. On the other hand, if you are making a darker or opaque garment, you can have fun with a contrasting or patterned lining.

Texture is also important. You want a lining fabric that feels good against the skin and won't cling or bunch up. Look for fabrics with a smooth or satin finish, or those that have been treated to resist static or wrinkles.

Choosing a Durable Fabric

While you want your lining fabric to be lightweight and comfortable, you also want it to be durable and long-lasting. This is especially important for garments that will be worn frequently or subjected to wear and tear, such as jackets or pants.

For these types of garments, I recommend using a lining fabric with a high thread count, such as a silk or cotton sateen. This will ensure that the lining holds up over time and doesn't fray or tear easily.

How to Choose a Cost-Effective Fabric

Finally, you want to choose a lining fabric that is affordable and cost-effective, especially if you are working on a budget. While silk and other high-end fabrics are beautiful and luxurious, they can also be expensive.

One option is to look for synthetic fabrics, such as polyester or nylon, that mimic the look and feel of more expensive fabrics. These can be a great choice for lining jackets, pants, and other structured garments.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I use the same fabric for the lining as I did for the outer garment?

A: While it is possible to use the same fabric for both the outer garment and the lining, it is generally not recommended. This can result in a bulky, heavy garment that doesn't drape well. It can also make it difficult to put the garment on and take it off.

Q: Can I use a knit fabric for lining?

A: Knit fabrics can be used as lining, but they are generally not recommended. Knit fabrics tend to stretch and move around more than woven fabrics, which can result in a misshapen or ill-fitting garment. Stick with lightweight woven fabrics for best results.

Q: Do I need to prewash my lining fabric?

A: Yes, it is always a good idea to prewash your lining fabric before using it. This will ensure that it doesn't shrink or bleed when you wash the finished garment. Be sure to follow the manufacturer's instructions for washing and drying.

Q: Can I use a different fabric for the lining than what is recommended in the pattern?

A: Yes, you can use a different fabric for the lining than what is recommended in the pattern. However, be aware that this may affect the overall look and feel of the garment, as well as its fit and durability. Always choose a lining fabric that is similar in weight, texture, and drape to what is recommended in the pattern.

Conclusion of What Fabric to Use as Lining

Choosing the right fabric to use as lining can make all the difference in the finished garment. By considering factors such as weight, color and texture, durability, and cost, you can make an informed decision that will result in a beautiful, comfortable, and long-lasting garment.
