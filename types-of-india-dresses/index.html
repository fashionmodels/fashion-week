Types Of Indian Dresses A Guide For Beginners

Written by Frank Sep 25, 2023 · 4 min read

Do you feel overwhelmed when it comes to choosing the right Indian dress for an occasion? Are you confused about the different types of Indian dresses available in the market? If yes, then you are not alone. Choosing the right Indian dress can be a daunting task, especially if you are new to Indian fashion. But don't worry, we are here to help!

Indian dresses are known for their vibrant colors, intricate designs, and beautiful fabrics. They are an integral part of Indian culture and are worn on various occasions such as weddings, festivals, and parties. However, with so many different types of Indian dresses available, it can be challenging to know which one to choose.

In this article, we will give you a comprehensive guide to different types of Indian dresses. We will discuss the history, target audience, and occasions where each dress is worn. So, let's get started!

Saree

Saree is one of the most popular Indian dresses and is worn by women of all ages. It is a long piece of cloth that is draped around the body and is usually worn with a blouse and petticoat. Sarees are available in a wide range of fabrics such as silk, cotton, chiffon, and georgette. They are worn on various occasions such as weddings, parties, and festivals.

My personal experience with saree was during my friend's wedding. I wore a beautiful red silk saree with intricate gold embroidery. It was a bit challenging to drape, but once I got the hang of it, I felt like a queen.

Salwar Kameez

Salwar Kameez is another popular Indian dress that is worn by women. It consists of a long tunic called kameez and loose-fitting pants called salwar. It is usually worn with a dupatta, which is a long scarf. Salwar Kameez is comfortable to wear and is perfect for everyday wear, as well as formal occasions.

I remember wearing a beautiful pink and gold Salwar Kameez for my cousin's engagement ceremony. It was comfortable to wear and allowed me to dance the night away.

Lehenga Choli

Lehenga Choli is a traditional Indian dress that is worn by women on special occasions such as weddings and festivals. It consists of a long skirt called lehenga, a short blouse called choli, and a dupatta. Lehenga Choli is usually made of silk or other rich fabrics and is adorned with intricate embroidery and embellishments.

How to Choose the Right Indian Dress?

Choosing the right Indian dress depends on several factors such as the occasion, your body type, and personal style. If you are attending a wedding or a formal event, then a saree or Lehenga Choli would be a good choice. If you are looking for something comfortable to wear on a daily basis, then a Salwar Kameez would be a great option.

Anarkali

Anarkali is a long, flowing dress that is inspired by the Mughal era. It consists of a long tunic that flares out at the bottom, leggings or churidar, and a dupatta. Anarkali is perfect for formal occasions such as weddings and parties.

My personal experience with Anarkali was during my sister's wedding. I wore a beautiful navy blue Anarkali with gold embroidery. It was comfortable to wear and made me feel like a princess.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear a saree if I am not Indian?

A: Yes, anyone can wear a saree regardless of their ethnicity or nationality.

Q: What is the difference between a saree and a lehenga?

A: A saree is a long piece of cloth that is draped around the body, while a lehenga is a skirt that is paired with a blouse and dupatta.

Q: Can I wear a Salwar Kameez to a wedding?

A: Yes, Salwar Kameez is a great option for weddings as it is comfortable to wear and looks elegant.

Q: What is the best fabric for Indian dresses?

A: The best fabric for Indian dresses depends on the occasion and personal preference. Silk, chiffon, and georgette are popular choices for weddings and formal events, while cotton and linen are great for everyday wear.

Conclusion of Types of Indian Dresses

Choosing the right Indian dress can be a daunting task but knowing the different types of Indian dresses can make the process easier. Whether you are attending a wedding, festival, or party, there is a perfect Indian dress for every occasion. Remember to choose a dress that makes you feel comfortable and confident, and you are sure to make a lasting impression.
