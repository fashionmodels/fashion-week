Best Fabric to Make Bathing Suits: What You Need to Know

Are you planning to make your own bathing suit but confused about what type of fabric to use? Choosing the right fabric for a bathing suit can be a daunting task, especially if you're not familiar with the different types of materials available. It's important to select a fabric that is not only comfortable, but also durable and able to withstand exposure to water, sun, and chlorine. In this post, we'll explore the best fabric to make bathing suits and help you make an informed decision.

When it comes to choosing the best fabric to make bathing suits, there's no one-size-fits-all answer. It really depends on your personal preferences and the type of activity you'll be doing while wearing the bathing suit. However, there are a few factors to consider when selecting a fabric:

1. Stretch: Look for a fabric that has at least 15-20% stretch. This will allow the bathing suit to move with you and provide a comfortable fit.

2. Durability: Make sure the fabric is able to withstand exposure to chemicals such as chlorine and salt water, as well as exposure to sun and heat.

3. Colorfastness: Choose a fabric that won't fade or lose its color after a few wears or washes.

4. Comfort: Select a fabric that is soft, breathable, and feels good against your skin.

Nylon/Spandex Blend

One of the most popular fabrics for making bathing suits is a nylon/spandex blend. This fabric is durable, stretchy, and comes in a variety of colors and prints. It's also lightweight and dries quickly, making it a great choice for water activities such as swimming or surfing. Personally, I have made several bathing suits using a nylon/spandex blend and have found it to be a reliable and comfortable option.

Polyester/Spandex Blend

Another popular fabric for making bathing suits is a polyester/spandex blend. This fabric is known for being chlorine-resistant, which is a great feature if you plan on swimming in a pool. It's also affordable and comes in a range of colors and prints. However, some people find polyester to be less comfortable than nylon, as it is not as soft or breathable.

Cotton/Spandex Blend

Cotton is a natural fiber that is soft and breathable, making it a comfortable option for a bathing suit. However, pure cotton is not ideal for swimwear as it absorbs water and takes a long time to dry. A cotton/spandex blend, on the other hand, provides the comfort of cotton with the added stretch of spandex. This fabric is great for lounging by the pool or beach, but may not be the best choice for high-intensity water activities.

How to Sew with Stretch Fabric

If you're new to sewing with stretch fabric, it can be a bit tricky to work with at first. Here are a few tips to help you get started:

1. Use a ballpoint needle: This type of needle is designed to prevent the fabric from getting damaged or snagged while sewing.

2. Use a stretch stitch: Most sewing machines have a stretch stitch setting which allows the fabric to stretch without breaking the thread.

3. Don't stretch the fabric while sewing: It's important to let the fabric feed through the machine naturally, without pulling or stretching it.

Conclusion of Best Fabric to Make Bathing Suits

Choosing the right fabric for a bathing suit can make all the difference in terms of comfort, durability, and overall performance. While there are many types of fabrics to choose from, a nylon/spandex or polyester/spandex blend are both great options for a bathing suit. Remember to consider factors such as stretch, durability, colorfastness, and comfort when selecting a fabric. And if you're new to sewing with stretch fabric, don't be intimidated! With a little practice, you'll be making your own custom bathing suits in no time.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I use a cotton/spandex blend for a one-piece bathing suit?

A: While a cotton/spandex blend is a comfortable option for a bathing suit, it may not be the best choice for a one-piece. One-piece bathing suits require a fabric that is able to hold its shape and provide support, and a cotton/spandex blend may not be able to do so.

Q: Should I pre-wash my fabric before sewing a bathing suit?

A: Yes, it's always a good idea to pre-wash your fabric before sewing. This will help to remove any shrinkage that may occur and prevent the fabric from changing shape or color after the first wash.

Q: How can I tell if a fabric is colorfast?

A: To test if a fabric is colorfast, dampen a small area of the fabric with water and rub it with a white cloth. If the color bleeds onto the cloth, the fabric is not colorfast and may bleed or fade over time.

Q: Can I use a different fabric for the lining of my bathing suit?

A: Yes, you can use a different fabric for the lining of your bathing suit. Just make sure the lining fabric is also stretchy and able to withstand exposure to water and heat.
