What to Expect During Knee Surgery?

During knee surgery, you will be asked to change into hospital gowns before the procedure. Once you are in the operating room, you will be asked to lie down on the surgical table. The surgical team will then administer anesthesia to help you sleep through the procedure. Depending on the type of surgery, the surgeon will make an incision in the knee and repair or replace the damaged joint.

After the surgery, you will be taken to the recovery room, where you will be monitored closely for any signs of complications. You will be given pain medication and instructions on how to care for your knee after surgery. You will be asked to wear a compression bandage or stocking to help reduce swelling and promote healing.

What to Wear During Knee Surgery?

As we mentioned earlier, you will be asked to change into hospital gowns before knee surgery. It is recommended to wear loose, comfortable clothing to the hospital on the day of surgery. You should avoid wearing tight-fitting clothes or jewelry, as they can interfere with the surgical procedure. You should also avoid wearing makeup or nail polish, as they can interfere with the anesthesia monitoring equipment.

Why Do You Have to Change into Hospital Gowns?

Changing into hospital gowns is a standard protocol for surgical procedures. Hospital gowns are designed to provide easy access to the surgical area and reduce the risk of infection. Hospital gowns are also made of sterile materials, which helps to maintain a sterile environment in the operating room. By changing into hospital gowns, you help to reduce the risk of infection and ensure the safety of yourself and others.

How to Prepare for Knee Surgery?

Preparing for knee surgery involves several steps, including:

1. Follow your doctor's instructions: Your doctor will provide you with instructions on how to prepare for knee surgery. Follow these instructions carefully to ensure the best possible outcome.

2. Arrange for transportation: You will not be able to drive after knee surgery, so make sure to arrange for transportation beforehand.

3. Prepare your home: Before surgery, make sure to prepare your home for your recovery. You might need to install grab bars, a shower chair, or a raised toilet seat.

4. Stock up on supplies: You will need to stock up on supplies such as ice packs, bandages, and medication before surgery.
