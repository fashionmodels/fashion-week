Men s Dress Shirt Sizes How To Measure

Written by Robby Jun 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

When it comes to dressing up for formal events, men can feel overwhelmed with the process of finding the right dress shirt size. It can be frustrating to go through the hassle of trying on different sizes or guessing which one will fit correctly. But fear not, measuring your dress shirt size is a simple process that can save you time and money in the long run.

Many men struggle with finding the right dress shirt size. It can be frustrating to buy a shirt that looks great on the hanger, only to find out it doesn't fit properly once you put it on. This can cause discomfort, restricted movement, and an overall unprofessional appearance.

The target of this tutorial is to help men understand how to measure their dress shirt size accurately to avoid these common issues.

In summary, this blog post will cover the following: What is men's dress shirt size, why it's important to measure your dress shirt size, how to measure your dress shirt size, and tips for finding the perfect fit.

What is Men's Dress Shirt Size?

Men's dress shirt sizes are determined by two measurements: neck size and sleeve length. Neck size refers to the circumference of the neck, while sleeve length is measured from the center of the back of the neck to the end of the sleeve. These measurements are usually listed in inches and can be found on the tag of the shirt.

Personally, I had trouble finding the right dress shirt size for years. I would often purchase a shirt that was too big or too small, and it wasn't until I learned how to measure my size that I finally found the perfect fit.

How to Measure Your Dress Shirt Size

To measure your dress shirt size, you will need a tape measure. Start by measuring your neck size. Wrap the tape measure around the base of your neck, where your collar would normally sit. Make sure to keep the tape measure snug, but not too tight. Round up to the nearest half-inch.

Next, measure your sleeve length. With your arms at your side, measure from the center of the back of your neck to the end of your shoulder. From there, measure from your shoulder to your wrist bone. Add these two numbers together to get your sleeve length. Round up to the nearest half-inch.

Tips for Finding the Perfect Fit

Once you have determined your neck size and sleeve length, it's important to find the right fit. A dress shirt should fit snugly around the neck without feeling tight. The sleeves should be long enough to reach your wrist bone, and the cuffs should fit snugly around your wrist. The shirt should be fitted around the chest and waist without feeling too tight or too loose.

Additional Tips:

- Try on different brands and styles to find the right fit for your body type
- Consider getting a custom-made shirt if you have trouble finding the right fit
- Don't be afraid to ask for help from a sales associate or tailor
- Make sure to wash and dry your shirt according to the care instructions to avoid shrinkage or stretching

Conclusion of Men's Dress Shirt Sizes: How to Measure

Measuring your dress shirt size is a simple process that can save you time and frustration in the long run. By following the tips and guidelines outlined in this tutorial, you can find the perfect fit for your body type and feel confident and comfortable in any formal setting.

Question and Answer

Q: What if my neck size and sleeve length fall between two sizes?

A: It's best to go with the larger size and have it taken in by a tailor if necessary.

Q: How can I measure my dress shirt size without a tape measure?

A: You can use a piece of string or a shoelace to measure your neck size and sleeve length. Simply measure the string or shoelace with a ruler to determine your size.

Q: Can I use the same size for all dress shirt brands?

A: Not necessarily. It's important to try on different brands and styles to find the right fit for your body type.

Q: Should a dress shirt be tucked in or left untucked?

A: It depends on the style of the shirt and the occasion. Generally, dress shirts are tucked in for formal events, while casual shirts can be left untucked.
