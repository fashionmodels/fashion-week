What Companies Make Green Hydrogen

Written by Oliver Oct 12, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you ever wondered what companies make green hydrogen? As the world becomes more conscious of the need to reduce carbon emissions, green hydrogen has emerged as a promising alternative to fossil fuels. However, not many people are familiar with the companies that produce this clean energy source.

Switching to green hydrogen is not just an environmental decision but also an economic one. The rising costs of fossil fuels and the increasing demand for clean energy make green hydrogen an attractive option for many industries.

So, what companies make green hydrogen? Let's find out.

Green hydrogen can be produced in a variety of ways, but the most common method is through the process of electrolysis. This involves splitting water molecules into hydrogen and oxygen using electricity from renewable sources like solar and wind. Some of the companies that produce green hydrogen using this method include Air Liquide, Linde, Siemens, and Nel Hydrogen.

Other companies like Plug Power, Ballard Power Systems, and ITM Power use proton exchange membrane (PEM) electrolysis to produce green hydrogen. This method is more efficient than traditional alkaline electrolysis and is better suited for smaller-scale applications.

In addition to electrolysis, green hydrogen can also be produced through the gasification of biomass or the reforming of methane with carbon capture and storage (CCS). Companies like Green Hydrogen Systems and Cummins specialize in these methods of production.

Overall, there are many companies that make green hydrogen, each with their own unique approach to production. As the world shifts towards a more sustainable future, we can expect to see more companies joining the green hydrogen movement.

What is Nel Hydrogen?

Nel Hydrogen is a Norwegian company that specializes in the production of green hydrogen. They offer a range of products and services that cater to various industries, including transportation, energy storage, and power-to-gas. Nel Hydrogen uses both alkaline and PEM electrolysis in their production process and has a global presence with offices in Europe, Asia, and North America.

Why is Green Hydrogen Important for the Environment?

Green hydrogen is important for the environment because it produces zero carbon emissions. This makes it a clean alternative to fossil fuels, which are a major contributor to climate change. Green hydrogen can be used in a variety of applications, including transportation, heating, and electricity generation. By using green hydrogen, we can reduce our carbon footprint and help mitigate the effects of climate change.

How Do Companies Produce Green Hydrogen?

Companies produce green hydrogen using a variety of methods, including electrolysis, gasification of biomass, and reforming of methane with CCS. Electrolysis is the most common method and involves splitting water molecules into hydrogen and oxygen using electricity from renewable sources like solar and wind. Gasification of biomass involves heating organic material in the absence of oxygen to produce a gas that can be converted into hydrogen. Reforming of methane with CCS involves converting natural gas into hydrogen and capturing the resulting carbon emissions for storage.

How Can I Invest in Companies that Make Green Hydrogen?

Investing in companies that make green hydrogen is a good way to support the transition to a more sustainable future while also potentially earning a profit. Some of the companies that produce green hydrogen are publicly traded, which means you can buy stocks in them. Some of the publicly traded companies in this industry include Air Liquide, Ballard Power Systems, and Plug Power.

Conclusion of What Companies Make Green Hydrogen

Green hydrogen is an emerging industry that holds great promise for a more sustainable future. Companies like Nel Hydrogen, Air Liquide, and Ballard Power Systems are leading the way in the production of this clean energy source. As the world becomes more conscious of the need to reduce carbon emissions, we can expect to see more companies joining the green hydrogen movement.
