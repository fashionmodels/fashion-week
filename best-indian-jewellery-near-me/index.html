The Different Types of Indian Jewellery

Indian jewellery is known for its intricate designs and stunning gemstones. There are several different types of Indian jewellery to choose from, each with its unique style and significance. Some of the most popular types of Indian jewellery include:

1. Kundan Jewellery

Kundan jewellery is made of gold and precious gemstones like diamonds, rubies, and emeralds. The gemstones are set using a technique called kundan, where the gems are set into a gold foil. Kundan jewellery is known for its elaborate and intricate designs and is typically worn for special occasions like weddings.

How to Style Kundan Jewellery

Kundan jewellery pairs well with traditional Indian outfits like sarees, lehengas, and salwar kameez. It's best to keep the rest of your accessories minimal, allowing the jewellery to be the focal point of your look.

2. Polki Jewellery

Polki jewellery is similar to kundan jewellery, but instead of using gold foil, the gems are set directly into the gold. Polki jewellery is known for its antique and vintage look and is typically more expensive than kundan jewellery.

How to Style Polki Jewellery

Polki jewellery looks great with traditional Indian outfits and can also be paired with modern western outfits for a fusion look.

3. Temple Jewellery

Temple jewellery is inspired by temple art and architecture and is typically made of gold. The jewellery features intricate designs of gods and goddesses, making it a popular choice for religious occasions.

How to Style Temple Jewellery

Temple jewellery pairs well with traditional Indian outfits like sarees, lehengas, and salwar kameez. You can also wear temple jewellery with a simple white shirt and jeans for a fusion look.

Tips for Buying Indian Jewellery

When it comes to buying Indian jewellery, there are a few things you should keep in mind. Here are some tips to help you make a confident purchase:

1. Do Your Research

Before making a purchase, do your research on the type of jewellery you want to buy. Look for reputable sellers and read reviews from other customers to ensure that you're getting a quality product.

2. Set a Budget

Indian jewellery can be expensive, so it's essential to set a budget before making a purchase. Determine how much you're willing to spend and stick to your budget to avoid overspending.

3. Check for Hallmarks

When buying gold jewellery, check for hallmarks to ensure that you're getting a quality product. The hallmark indicates the purity of the gold and is typically located on the clasp or the back of the jewellery piece.
