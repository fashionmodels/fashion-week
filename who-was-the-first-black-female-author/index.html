Who Was Phillis Wheatley?

Phillis Wheatley was born in West Africa in the 1750s and was kidnapped and brought to America as a slave. Despite her difficult circumstances, Wheatley showed an early talent for writing poetry. She was eventually purchased by the Wheatley family of Boston, who recognized her talent and provided her with an education. In 1773, Wheatley published a book of poetry called "Poems on Various Subjects, Religious and Moral," which made her the first African American and the first woman of African descent to publish a book of poetry in the United States.

The Legacy of Phillis Wheatley

Phillis Wheatley's work had a significant impact on the literary world, as it challenged the prevailing stereotypes about black people and their intellectual abilities. Phillis Wheatley's Poetry
