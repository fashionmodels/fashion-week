What to Wear to a White Person's Wedding: A Guide for Non-White Guests

Weddings are already nerve-wracking events, but being a guest at a white person's wedding can add a whole new level of stress, especially when it comes to the dress code. As a non-white guest, you may be wondering what to wear to a white person's wedding without feeling out of place or disrespectful.

The first thing to keep in mind when figuring out what to wear to a white person's wedding is the dress code. Typically, the invitation will give you some indication of the dress code, whether it's black tie, semi-formal, or casual. If the invitation is unclear or you're still unsure, it's always better to err on the side of caution and dress more formally than too casually.

In terms of what to wear specifically, it's best to avoid anything too revealing or flashy. This means no short hemlines, low-cut tops, or bright colors. Instead, opt for a more conservative and understated look, such as a knee-length dress or a suit. It's also a good idea to avoid wearing all white, as this can be seen as trying to upstage the bride.

In summary, when deciding what to wear to a white person's wedding, it's important to first consider the dress code and then choose a conservative and understated outfit that avoids anything too revealing or flashy.

What to Wear to a White Person's Wedding: Personal Experience

As a person of color who has attended several white weddings, I've learned a few things about what to wear. One time, I made the mistake of wearing a bright red dress to a semi-formal wedding, only to realize upon arrival that all the other guests were wearing more muted colors. I felt out of place and uncomfortable for the rest of the night. Another time, I wore a knee-length black dress to a black tie wedding, only to be underdressed compared to the other guests in full-length gowns and tuxedos.

Based on these experiences, I've learned that it's better to err on the side of caution and dress more formally than too casually. It's also important to pay attention to the color and style of your outfit to avoid standing out too much or being too underdressed.

What to Wear to a White Person's Wedding: Accessorizing

When figuring out what to wear to a white person's wedding, don't forget about accessories. It's important to choose accessories that complement your outfit without overpowering it. For women, this might mean opting for simple jewelry and a clutch instead of a large statement necklace and a bulky purse. For men, this might mean choosing a classic tie and pocket square instead of a brightly patterned one.

What to Wear to a White Person's Wedding: Shoes

Shoes are another important consideration when deciding what to wear to a white person's wedding. For women, it's best to avoid anything too high or too casual, such as flip-flops or sneakers. Instead, opt for a classic pump or dressy sandal. For men, leather dress shoes are the way to go, whether they're oxfords, loafers, or brogues.

What to Wear to a White Person's Wedding: Hair and Makeup

Finally, when it comes to hair and makeup, it's best to keep things simple and classic. Avoid anything too trendy or bold, and instead choose a more timeless look. This might mean a simple updo or sleek blowout for women, and a well-groomed hairstyle for men. In terms of makeup, a natural and polished look is always a safe bet.

What to Wear to a White Person's Wedding: Personal Experience

At one wedding I attended, I made the mistake of trying out a bold new hairstyle that didn't quite work out. I felt self-conscious and wished I had stuck with my usual look. Another time, I opted for a trendy makeup look that ended up clashing with my outfit. I learned from these experiences that it's best to stick with what you know works for you, rather than trying anything too new or risky.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear a pantsuit to a white person's wedding?

A: Yes, a pantsuit can be a great option for women as long as it's dressy enough for the occasion. Look for a suit made from a dressy fabric like silk or velvet, and pair it with heels and simple jewelry.

Q: Can I wear jeans to a white person's wedding?

A: No, jeans are generally not appropriate for a wedding, even if it's a more casual affair. Opt for dress pants or a dressy skirt instead.

Q: Can I wear a traditional outfit from my culture to a white person's wedding?

A: It depends on the dress code and the couple's preferences. If the dress code is formal, it's best to opt for a more Western-style outfit. However, if the dress code is more casual or if the couple has expressed an interest in seeing traditional outfits, it may be appropriate to wear one.

Q: Can I wear a hat to a white person's wedding?

A: It depends on the type of hat and the dress code. For women, a small fascinator or headband can be a great addition to an outfit, but a large hat is generally not appropriate unless it's a garden party or outdoor wedding. For men, a classic fedora or other dressy hat can be a good choice, but it's best to avoid anything too casual or trendy.

Conclusion of What to Wear to a White Person's Wedding

When it comes to what to wear to a white person's wedding, the key is to choose a conservative and understated outfit that complies with the dress code and doesn't draw too much attention to yourself. Pay attention to the color and style of your outfit, as well as your accessories, shoes, and hair and makeup. By following these guidelines, you'll be sure to feel comfortable and confident at any white person's wedding.
