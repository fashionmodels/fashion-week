Tailored Fit Vs Traditional Fit Shirts Jos A Bank Which One Is Right For You

Written by Daniel Sep 16, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of buying dress shirts that don't fit you properly? Do you find that the sleeves are too long, the collar is too loose, or the shirt is too baggy? These are common pain points that many men face when shopping for dress shirts. Fortunately, Jos A Bank offers two different fits for their dress shirts: tailored fit and traditional fit. But which one is right for you?

The target of tailored fit vs traditional fit shirts Jos A Bank is to help men find the perfect dress shirt that fits their body type and personal style. Both fits have their own unique features and benefits, so it's important to understand the differences between them to make an informed decision.

In summary, tailored fit shirts are designed to fit closer to the body, with a slimmer cut through the chest, waist, and hips. They have higher armholes and narrower sleeves for a more modern, streamlined look. On the other hand, traditional fit shirts are cut more generously, with a fuller cut through the chest, waist, and hips. They have lower armholes and fuller sleeves for a classic, comfortable fit.

The Target of Tailored Fit vs Traditional Fit Shirts Jos A Bank

As someone who has struggled with finding dress shirts that fit properly, I can definitely relate to the frustration of trying on shirt after shirt, only to be disappointed by the fit. When I discovered Jos A Bank's tailored fit and traditional fit options, I was curious to try them out and see which one would work best for me.

After trying on both fits, I found that the tailored fit was a better option for me. I have a slim build, and the tailored fit hugged my body in all the right places without feeling too tight or restrictive. The sleeves were the perfect length, and the collar fit comfortably without gaping or feeling too loose.

The Benefits of Tailored Fit vs Traditional Fit Shirts Jos A Bank

One of the benefits of the tailored fit is that it gives a more modern, stylish look. The slimmer cut through the chest and waist creates a sleeker silhouette that is more flattering for men with a leaner build. The higher armholes and narrower sleeves also give a more tailored look that is popular among younger men.

On the other hand, the traditional fit is a great option for men who prefer a more classic, comfortable fit. The fuller cut through the chest and waist provides more room to move around and breathe, which can be especially beneficial for men with a larger build. The lower armholes and fuller sleeves also provide a more relaxed, casual look that is perfect for everyday wear.

Which One Should You Choose?

The answer to this question really depends on your personal preference and body type. If you have a slim build and prefer a more modern, tailored look, then the tailored fit may be the better option for you. However, if you have a larger build or prefer a more classic, comfortable fit, then the traditional fit may be a better choice.

How to Find the Perfect Fit

When shopping for dress shirts, it's important to take accurate measurements of your body to ensure a proper fit. Measure your neck, chest, waist, and hips, and compare them to the sizing chart provided by the brand. It's also a good idea to try on different sizes and fits to see which one feels the most comfortable and flattering on your body.
