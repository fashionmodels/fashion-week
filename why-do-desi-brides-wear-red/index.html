Why Do Desi Brides Wear Red

Written by Eveline Nov 02, 2023 · 5 min read

Weddings are a significant event in every culture, and Indian weddings are no exception. From the vibrant decorations to the aromatic food, Indian weddings are an experience in themselves. However, one question that often arises is why do desi brides wear red on their wedding day?

Indian weddings are a grand affair, and the bride's attire is an essential part of the celebration. However, choosing the perfect wedding outfit is not an easy task, especially for the bride. The bride has to consider various factors such as the color, style, and the cultural significance of the dress.

The answer to the question of why do desi brides wear red is simple. In Indian culture, red is considered an auspicious color. It symbolizes love, purity, and prosperity. Red is also believed to ward off evil spirits and bring good luck to the newlyweds.

In addition to this, red is also associated with the Hindu goddess of marriage, Durga. As a result, brides often opt for a red wedding dress, as it is believed to bring blessings from the goddess.

To summarize, desi brides wear red on their wedding day because of the cultural significance associated with the color. Red symbolizes love, purity, and prosperity, and is believed to bring good luck and blessings to the newlyweds. It is also associated with the Hindu goddess of marriage, Durga.

Why Do Desi Brides Wear Red - The Significance

As an Indian bride, I always knew that I wanted to wear red on my wedding day. For me, it was not just a choice of color but a way of paying homage to my culture and traditions.

The significance of red in Indian weddings is deep-rooted. It is not just a color but a symbol of love, purity, and prosperity. Red is believed to bring good luck, ward off evil spirits, and protect the newlyweds from harm.

In addition to this, red is also associated with the Hindu goddess of marriage, Durga. The color is believed to bring blessings from the goddess and ensure a happy and successful marriage.

As an Indian bride, wearing red on my wedding day was a way of honoring my culture and traditions. It was a way of embracing my roots and celebrating the rich cultural heritage of India.

Why Do Desi Brides Wear Red - The Wedding Attire

The traditional Indian wedding attire for brides is a red saree or lehenga. The outfit is usually adorned with intricate embroidery, sequins, and beads, making it a stunning piece of art.

The red wedding dress is not just a fashion statement but a symbol of the bride's purity, love, and prosperity. It is a way of displaying the bride's beauty and elegance while also showcasing the cultural significance of the color.

Moreover, the red wedding dress is often complemented with gold jewelry, which further enhances its beauty and grandeur. Gold is also considered an auspicious metal in Indian culture and is believed to bring good luck and prosperity.

Why Do Desi Brides Wear Red - The Rituals

Indian weddings are not just about the wedding day but the rituals that precede it. The bride and groom undergo various pre-wedding rituals, which hold great significance in Indian culture.

One such ritual is the haldi ceremony, where the bride is smeared with turmeric paste to bring out her inner glow. The bride typically wears a yellow outfit for this ceremony, as yellow is believed to symbolize happiness and prosperity.

However, on the wedding day, the bride wears red to symbolize the beginning of a new phase in her life. It is a way of proclaiming her love and commitment to her partner and celebrating the start of a new chapter in her life.

Why Do Desi Brides Wear Red - How To Choose The Perfect Outfit

Choosing the perfect wedding outfit is not an easy task, especially for Indian brides. With so many options available, it can be overwhelming to decide on the perfect dress.

Here are a few tips to help you choose the perfect red wedding outfit:

  • Consider your body type and choose a dress that flatters your figure.
  • Choose a dress that reflects your personal style and personality.
  • Consider the season and the wedding theme when choosing your outfit.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and designs.

Why Do Desi Brides Wear Red - Question and Answer

Q: Is it mandatory for Indian brides to wear red on their wedding day?

A: No, it is not mandatory for Indian brides to wear red on their wedding day. However, red is the traditional color for Indian brides and is considered auspicious.

Q: Can Indian brides wear other colors besides red?

A: Yes, Indian brides can wear other colors besides red. However, the color should be chosen based on personal preference and cultural significance.

Q: What is the significance of gold jewelry in Indian weddings?

A: Gold jewelry is considered auspicious in Indian culture and is believed to bring good luck and prosperity. It is often worn by brides on their wedding day as a symbol of their wealth and status.

Q: Why is the haldi ceremony significant in Indian weddings?

A: The haldi ceremony is significant in Indian weddings as it is believed to bring out the bride's inner glow and purify her body. It is also a way of celebrating the bride's upcoming wedding and showering her with love and blessings.

Conclusion of Why Do Desi Brides Wear Red

The red wedding dress is not just a fashion statement but a symbol of love, purity, and prosperity. It is a way of paying homage to Indian culture and traditions and celebrating the rich cultural heritage of India. The significance of red in Indian weddings is deep-rooted and holds great importance in Indian culture.
