Why Do Indian Brides Wear Red Bangles

Written by Luffy Aug 19, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Indian weddings are known for their vibrant colors and stunning jewelry. One of the most iconic pieces of jewelry worn by Indian brides are red bangles. But have you ever wondered why do Indian brides wear red bangles? In this article, we will explore the significance behind this tradition.

For many brides, their wedding day is one of the most important days of their lives. It is a day filled with joy, excitement, and anticipation. However, it can also be a day filled with anxiety and stress. From the wedding planning to the actual ceremony, there are many things that can cause stress for the bride. This is where the tradition of wearing red bangles comes in. Red is considered to be a auspicious color in Indian culture and is believed to ward off evil spirits. The red bangles are said to bring good luck and protect the bride from any harm.

Aside from their protective qualities, red bangles also have a cultural significance. In Indian culture, bangles are considered to be a symbol of femininity and marital status. They are worn by married women as a sign of their commitment to their husbands. The red color of the bangles symbolizes love and prosperity.

In summary, the tradition of wearing red bangles on their wedding day is a way for Indian brides to protect themselves from harm and show their commitment to their husbands. It is also a way to bring good luck and prosperity into their new lives together.

Why Red Bangles?

As mentioned earlier, red is considered to be an auspicious color in Indian culture. It is believed to bring good luck and ward off evil spirits. Red bangles are often worn by brides as part of their wedding ensemble to bring good luck and protect them from any harm. The red color is also symbolic of love and prosperity, which are two important aspects of a successful marriage.

When I got married, I wore red bangles as part of my wedding jewelry. It was a way for me to connect with my cultural roots and honor the traditions of my ancestors. I also felt a sense of comfort knowing that the bangles would protect me from any harm on my special day.

The Significance of Bangles in Indian Culture

Bangles are an important part of Indian culture and are worn by women of all ages. They are considered to be a symbol of femininity and marital status. Married women wear bangles as a sign of their commitment to their husbands, while unmarried women wear them for fashion purposes.

In addition to their symbolic meaning, bangles also have a practical purpose. They are believed to help improve blood circulation in the wrist and arm area. This is why pregnant women in India are often advised to wear bangles during their pregnancy.

The Different Types of Bangles

There are many different styles of bangles in India, each with their own unique meaning. Some of the most popular types include:

  • Gold Bangles: These are the most traditional type of bangles and are often worn by brides.
  • Glass Bangles: These are colorful bangles made of glass and are popular among young girls.
  • Lac Bangles: These are bangles made of lac and are often embellished with stones or beads.

How to Choose the Right Bangles

Choosing the right bangles can be a daunting task, especially for brides. Here are some tips to help you choose the perfect bangles for your wedding day:

  • Consider the color of your outfit: Choose bangles that complement the color of your outfit.
  • Choose the right size: Make sure the bangles fit comfortably on your wrist.
  • Consider the style: Choose bangles that match your personal style and the overall theme of your wedding.

My Personal Experience with Red Bangles

As an Indian woman, wearing red bangles on my wedding day was a no-brainer. It was a way for me to connect with my cultural heritage and honor the traditions of my ancestors. I felt a sense of pride and comfort wearing the bangles, knowing that they would protect me from any harm on my special day.

Question and Answer

Q: Can non-Indian brides wear red bangles?

A: While red bangles are traditionally worn by Indian brides, there is no rule that says non-Indian brides can't wear them. If you feel a connection to the tradition and want to honor it, then go ahead and wear them!

Q: Can I wear other colors besides red?

A: Yes, you can wear bangles in any color you want. However, if you want to honor the traditional significance of bangles, then red is the most auspicious color to wear.

Q: Can I wear bangles on other occasions besides my wedding?

A: Absolutely! Bangles are worn by Indian women on a daily basis and can be worn on any occasion. Just make sure to choose the right style and color for the occasion.

Q: Do I have to wear bangles on both wrists?

A: No, you can wear bangles on one wrist or both. It's up to you!

Conclusion of Why Do Indian Brides Wear Red Bangles

Wearing red bangles on their wedding day is a tradition that has been passed down through generations of Indian brides. It is a way for them to protect themselves from harm, show their commitment to their husbands, and bring good luck and prosperity into their new lives together. While the tradition may seem simple, it is deeply rooted in Indian culture and is a symbol of the rich heritage and traditions of the country.
