What Is Japanese Traditional Clothing Called

Written by Luffy May 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Japan has a rich history and culture, and one of the most notable aspects of this is their traditional clothing. From the intricate patterns to the unique designs, Japanese traditional clothing is a sight to behold. If you're curious about what is Japanese traditional clothing called, keep reading to learn more.

For many people, traditional clothing can be a source of confusion. With different names and styles, it can be difficult to keep track of everything. Plus, if you're not familiar with Japanese culture, it can be even more daunting. But don't worry, we're here to help.

So, what is Japanese traditional clothing called? The answer is quite simple: it's called "kimono". While many people may recognize the name, there are still some misconceptions about what a kimono actually is.

A kimono is a traditional Japanese garment that is typically made from silk or cotton. It consists of a T-shaped robe that is wrapped around the body, with the left side being placed over the right side. The kimono is held in place with a sash called an "obi", which is tied around the waist. Kimonos can be worn by both men and women, although the designs and patterns may differ.

History of Kimono

Kimono has a long and rich history in Japan. The first kimonos were made during the Heian period (794-1185), and they were originally worn as undergarments. Over time, they became more popular and were eventually worn as outerwear. In the Edo period (1603-1868), kimonos became even more popular and were worn by people of all social classes.

Today, kimonos are typically worn for special occasions, such as weddings, graduations, and coming-of-age ceremonies. They are also popular among tourists who want to experience traditional Japanese culture.

Types of Kimono

There are several different types of kimonos, each with their own unique designs and patterns. Some of the most popular types include:

Furisode

This is a type of kimono that is typically worn by young, unmarried women. It features long sleeves that hang down to the knees and is often adorned with bold, colorful designs.

Hakama

The hakama is a type of kimono that is worn by both men and women. It features wide, flowing pants that are held up by a sash. The hakama is typically worn for martial arts or other formal occasions.

Yukata

The yukata is a casual type of kimono that is typically worn during the summer months. It is made from lightweight cotton and features simple, understated designs.

Tomesode

This is a type of kimono that is typically worn by married women. It features short sleeves and is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Personal Experience

When I visited Japan a few years ago, I had the opportunity to try on a kimono. It was an amazing experience to wear such a beautiful and intricate garment. The obi was quite difficult to tie, but with the help of the shop owner, I was able to get it just right. I felt like a princess walking around in my kimono, and it was truly a highlight of my trip.

How to Wear a Kimono

If you're interested in wearing a kimono, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, make sure you choose the right size. Kimonos are typically one-size-fits-all, but you'll want to make sure it fits you properly. Next, learn how to tie an obi. This can be quite challenging, so it's a good idea to seek help from a professional or watch some tutorials online. Finally, practice walking in your kimono. The long sleeves and flowing fabric can make it difficult to move around, so make sure you're comfortable before you head out.

Conclusion of What is Japanese Traditional Clothing Called

Now that you know what Japanese traditional clothing is called, you can appreciate the beauty and complexity of this amazing garment. Whether you're interested in wearing a kimono or simply admiring them from afar, they are a true representation of Japanese culture and history.
