The History of Italian Watchmaking

Italian watchmaking dates back to the 16th century, when craftsmen in Milan and Florence began creating ornate pocket watches for the wealthy elite. Over time, Italian watchmakers developed a reputation for innovation and creativity, with many of them pioneering new techniques and designs.

In the 20th century, Italian watchmaking experienced a resurgence thanks to brands like Panerai and U-Boat. These companies focused on creating rugged, military-inspired watches that could withstand the toughest conditions. Today, Italian watchmaking is thriving, with a wide range of brands producing everything from classic dress watches to cutting-edge smartwatches.
