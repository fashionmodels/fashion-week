How To Buy Cowgirl Boots A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Sep 30, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking to buy your first pair of cowgirl boots but don't know where to start? You're not alone. With so many options to choose from, it can be overwhelming to navigate the world of western footwear. But fear not, we've got you covered with this step-by-step guide on how to buy cowgirl boots.

One of the biggest pain points when it comes to buying cowgirl boots is finding the right fit. With so many styles and sizes available, it can be difficult to know which ones will work for you. Additionally, some boots can be quite expensive, so you want to make sure you're investing in a pair that will last and be comfortable to wear.

First things first, know your foot size. This may sound obvious, but it's important to measure your feet before buying any type of shoe, especially cowgirl boots. Western boots tend to run a bit large, so you may need to go down a half size or even a full size smaller than your regular shoe size. Once you have your measurements, you can start looking for boots that will fit you properly.

When shopping for cowgirl boots, keep in mind the purpose you will be using them for. Are you planning to wear them for fashion purposes or for horseback riding? This will affect the style of boots you choose. Fashion boots typically have a pointed toe and a higher heel, while riding boots have a rounded toe and a lower heel.

Choosing the Right Material and Design

Next, consider the material and design of the boots. Leather is the most common material used for cowgirl boots, but you can also find them in suede or exotic skins such as alligator or snake. When it comes to design, there are endless options to choose from. From intricate embroidery to colorful designs, cowgirl boots can be as unique as the person wearing them.

One personal experience to keep in mind is breaking in your new boots. Leather boots need time to soften and mold to your feet, so they may feel stiff and uncomfortable at first. To help break them in, wear them around the house for short periods of time and gradually increase the wear time until they feel comfortable.

Where to Buy Cowgirl Boots

Now that you know what to look for in a pair of cowgirl boots, where can you find them? Online retailers such as Amazon and Zappos offer a wide variety of styles and brands to choose from. If you prefer to try on boots in person, western wear stores such as Boot Barn and Cavender's are great options. These stores typically have knowledgeable staff who can help you find the perfect pair of boots and offer advice on fit and care.

Caring for Your Cowgirl Boots

Proper care and maintenance is key to making your cowgirl boots last. Leather boots should be cleaned and conditioned regularly to prevent cracking and dryness. Store them in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent fading and damage.

Final Thoughts on How to Buy Cowgirl Boots

Buying a pair of cowgirl boots can be a fun and exciting experience, but it can also be overwhelming. Remember to measure your feet, consider the purpose and design of the boots, and choose a reputable retailer to ensure you're getting a quality product. With these tips in mind, you'll be strutting your stuff in your new cowgirl boots in no time!

Question and Answer

Q: How do I know if I need a pointed or rounded toe on my cowgirl boots?

A: It depends on the purpose you will be using them for. Pointed toe boots are more fashionable and better suited for dressy occasions, while rounded toe boots are more practical for horseback riding and everyday wear.

Q: What is the best way to break in new cowgirl boots?

A: Wear them around the house for short periods of time and gradually increase the wear time until they feel comfortable. You can also try using leather conditioner to soften the leather.

Q: How often should I clean and condition my cowgirl boots?

A: It's recommended to clean and condition leather boots every 3-6 months, depending on how often you wear them.

Q: Can I wear my cowgirl boots in the rain?

A: It's not recommended to wear leather boots in the rain, as water can damage the leather and cause them to warp or crack. If you must wear them in wet conditions, be sure to apply a water-resistant spray to protect them.

Conclusion of How to Buy Cowgirl Boots

Buying cowgirl boots doesn't have to be a daunting task. By following these simple steps, you can find a pair of boots that not only look great, but are comfortable and practical as well. Remember to measure your feet, consider the purpose and design of the boots, and choose a reputable retailer. Happy boot shopping!
