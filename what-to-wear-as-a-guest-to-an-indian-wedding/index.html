What To Wear As A Guest To An Indian Wedding A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Aug 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Attending an Indian wedding as a guest is an exciting and unforgettable experience. However, figuring out what to wear can be a daunting task, especially if you're not familiar with Indian culture and fashion. You don't want to end up feeling out of place or offending anyone with your outfit. In this guide, we'll help you navigate the dress code for Indian weddings and provide tips on what to wear as a guest.

If you're attending an Indian wedding for the first time, you might be wondering what the dress code is. Indian weddings are typically colorful, vibrant, and grand affairs that require guests to dress accordingly. The dress code for Indian weddings is usually traditional attire, such as sarees, lehengas, salwar kameez, or fusion wear. Men can opt for traditional attire like kurta pajamas or Indo-western outfits.

The key to dressing appropriately for an Indian wedding is to strike a balance between being stylish and respectful of the culture's traditions. Here are some tips on what to wear as a guest to an Indian wedding:

The Saree: A Timeless Classic

When in doubt, a saree is always a safe bet. A saree is a long, flowing garment that is draped around the body and comes in a variety of fabrics, colors, and styles. It is a timeless classic that never goes out of style and is perfect for formal occasions like weddings. Pair it with traditional jewelry and a clutch to complete the look.

Lehenga: A Regal Choice

A lehenga is a skirt that is paired with a crop top or blouse and a dupatta. It is a popular choice for Indian weddings, especially for the bride and her bridesmaids. As a guest, you can opt for a lehenga in a bold color or with intricate embroidery. Make sure to pair it with statement jewelry and heels.

Salwar Kameez: Comfort Meets Style

If you're looking for something comfortable yet stylish, a salwar kameez is a great option. It is a three-piece outfit that consists of a loose-fitting tunic, pants, and a scarf or dupatta. You can choose from a variety of styles, such as Anarkali, Punjabi, or straight-cut. Pair it with traditional jhumkas and sandals for a complete look.

Kurta Pajama: A Classic Choice for Men

Men can opt for a classic kurta pajama, which is a long tunic paired with loose-fitting pants. It is a popular choice for Indian weddings and can be dressed up with a jacket or Nehru vest. Make sure to choose a fabric that is lightweight and breathable, especially if the wedding is during the summer months.

Fusion Wear: A Modern Twist

If you're not comfortable with traditional attire, you can opt for fusion wear that combines Western and Indian elements. For women, this could mean pairing a crop top with a skirt or wearing a jumpsuit with a dupatta. Men can opt for Indo-western outfits that combine a Western-style jacket with traditional pants. Just make sure that the outfit is appropriate for a formal occasion like a wedding.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Can I wear black to an Indian wedding?
A: It's best to avoid wearing black as it is considered inauspicious in Indian culture. Opt for bright colors instead.

Q: Do I need to cover my head?
A: It depends on the dress code and the traditions of the wedding. Some weddings require women to cover their heads with a dupatta or shawl, while others don't.

Q: Can I wear jeans to an Indian wedding?
A: It's best to avoid wearing jeans to an Indian wedding as it is considered too casual. Opt for traditional attire or fusion wear instead.

Q: Can I wear Western-style dresses to an Indian wedding?
A: It depends on the dress code and the traditions of the wedding. Some weddings allow Western-style dresses, while others require traditional attire.

Conclusion of What to Wear as a Guest to an Indian Wedding

Attending an Indian wedding as a guest is a wonderful experience that requires dressing appropriately. Whether you opt for traditional attire or fusion wear, make sure that your outfit is respectful of the culture's traditions and is appropriate for a formal occasion. With these tips, you'll be able to navigate the dress code for Indian weddings and look your best.
