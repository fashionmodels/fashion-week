Is It Better To Not Wear Underwear When You Have A Uti

Written by Christine May 23, 2023 · 4 min read

UTIs, or urinary tract infections, are common and uncomfortable infections that can cause pain and discomfort. Many people wonder if they should stop wearing underwear when they have a UTI, as they believe that it may help alleviate some of the symptoms. In this article, we will explore the topic and provide you with the information you need to make an informed decision.

UTIs can cause a variety of symptoms, including painful urination, frequent urination, and lower abdominal pain. Many people experience discomfort when wearing underwear, as it can cause irritation and pressure in the infected area. However, it is important to note that not wearing underwear may not be the best solution for everyone.

If you are experiencing discomfort when wearing underwear, it may be beneficial to try going without it for a short period of time to see if it helps alleviate your symptoms. However, if you are experiencing leakage or urinary incontinence, it may be more comfortable to wear underwear for protection.

In summary, whether or not to wear underwear when you have a UTI is a personal decision that depends on your individual situation. It is important to listen to your body and do what is most comfortable for you.

Pros of Not Wearing Underwear When You Have a UTI

Not wearing underwear when you have a UTI can help alleviate discomfort and irritation in the infected area. It can also promote better airflow, which can help keep the area dry and reduce the risk of developing a secondary infection.

Cons of Not Wearing Underwear When You Have a UTI

Not wearing underwear when you have a UTI can increase the risk of leakage and urinary incontinence, which can cause embarrassment and discomfort. It can also be uncomfortable for some people to not wear underwear, especially if they are used to wearing it regularly.

If you decide to not wear underwear when you have a UTI, it is important to properly care for yourself to reduce the risk of developing a secondary infection. This includes keeping the area clean and dry, wearing loose-fitting clothing, and avoiding activities that may cause friction or irritation in the infected area.
