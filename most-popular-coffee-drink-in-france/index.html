The Most Popular Coffee Drink In France A Beginner s Guide

Written by Christine Sep 11, 2023 · 5 min read

France is known for its love of coffee, but navigating the world of French coffee can be overwhelming for those who are new to it. From the strong espresso shots to the creamy café au lait, there are many options to choose from. In this guide, we will explore the most popular coffee drink in France and provide you with all the information you need to order like a local.

When it comes to coffee in France, the most popular drink is the café au lait. This drink is made with equal parts espresso and steamed milk, and it is typically served in a large bowl-like cup. Café au lait is perfect for those who prefer a milder coffee taste, as the milk helps to cut the bitterness of the espresso.

To order a café au lait in France, simply ask for "un café au lait, s'il vous plaît." If you would like to customize your drink, you can ask for it to be made with more or less milk, or with a shot of flavored syrup.

What is a Café au Lait?

When I first visited France, I was overwhelmed by the coffee options available. As someone who usually drinks coffee with milk, I was drawn to the café au lait. This drink quickly became my go-to order, and I found that it was the perfect way to start my mornings.

A café au lait is made with equal parts espresso and steamed milk. The milk helps to cut the bitterness of the espresso, resulting in a milder coffee taste. It is typically served in a large bowl-like cup, and it is often accompanied by a croissant or a pain au chocolat.

How to Order a Café au Lait in France

Ordering a café au lait in France is easy. Simply ask for "un café au lait, s'il vous plaît." If you would like to customize your drink, you can ask for it to be made with more or less milk, or with a shot of flavored syrup.

It is important to note that in France, coffee is typically enjoyed in the morning and afternoon. If you order a café au lait in the evening, you may receive some confused looks from the locals.

The History of Coffee in France

Coffee was introduced to France in the 17th century, and it quickly gained popularity among the aristocracy. In the 18th century, coffeehouses began to pop up in Paris, and they became a gathering place for intellectuals and artists.

Today, coffee is an important part of French culture. It is typically enjoyed in the morning and afternoon, and it is often accompanied by a croissant or a pain au chocolat.

How to Make a Café au Lait at Home

If you want to make a café au lait at home, all you need is an espresso machine and some steamed milk. Simply pull a shot of espresso and add it to a bowl-like cup. Then, steam some milk and add it to the cup. Stir to combine, and enjoy!

Where to Find the Best Café au Lait in France

There are many places to find a great café au lait in France. Some of the best cafés can be found in Paris, but there are also great options in smaller cities and towns.

One of my favorite places to enjoy a café au lait is at a local patisserie. These small shops often have a cozy atmosphere and delicious pastries to accompany your coffee.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the difference between a café au lait and a latte?

A: A café au lait is made with equal parts espresso and steamed milk, while a latte is made with more milk than espresso.

Q: Can I order a café au lait in the evening?

A: While you can technically order a café au lait in the evening, it is not as common as ordering it in the morning or afternoon.

Q: What is the most popular coffee chain in France?

A: The most popular coffee chain in France is Starbucks, but there are many independent cafés that are worth exploring.

Q: Can I customize my café au lait?

A: Yes, you can ask for your café au lait to be made with more or less milk, or with a shot of flavored syrup.

Conclusion of Most Popular Coffee Drink in France

If you are visiting France for the first time, ordering coffee can be intimidating. However, the café au lait is a great place to start. This drink is easy to order, and it is perfect for those who prefer a milder coffee taste. Whether you enjoy it at a local café or make it at home, a café au lait is a delicious way to experience French coffee culture.
