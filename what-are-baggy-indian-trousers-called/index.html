What Are Baggy Indian Trousers Called

Written by Oliver Aug 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered what those loose, comfortable pants that are commonly worn in India are called? They're often seen as a fashion statement and have become increasingly popular in recent years. If you're curious about what these pants are called and why they're so popular, keep reading!

Pain Points Related to Baggy Indian Trousers

For those who are not familiar with Indian clothing, it can be challenging to identify specific garments. This can make it difficult to shop for or discuss these clothes with others. Additionally, there are multiple types of baggy pants in India, which can be confusing for those who are not well-versed in Indian fashion.

What Are Baggy Indian Trousers Called?

The baggy Indian trousers that we're referring to are called "harem pants" or "dhoti pants." They are loose, flowy pants that taper at the ankles and are typically made from lightweight fabrics like cotton or silk. These pants are often worn with kurtas or tunics and are a popular choice for both casual and formal occasions.

While the term "harem pants" may sound unfamiliar or even offensive to some, it's simply a name given to this style of pants due to their resemblance to traditional harem attire. On the other hand, "dhoti pants" are named after the dhoti, a garment worn by men in India that is similar to a long loincloth.

The Versatility of Baggy Indian Trousers

One of the reasons why harem pants and dhoti pants have become so popular is their versatility. They can be dressed up or down, depending on the occasion. For example, you can pair them with a crop top and sandals for a casual look, or dress them up with a blouse and heels for a formal event.

Another reason why these pants are so popular is their comfort. They're perfect for hot weather, as they allow air to circulate around your legs, keeping you cool and comfortable. Additionally, their loose fit means that you can move around with ease, making them a great choice for activities like yoga or dance.

The History of Baggy Indian Trousers

The history of harem pants can be traced back to the Middle East, where they were worn by both men and women in the Ottoman Empire. They were designed to be comfortable and practical for everyday wear, and their loose fit made them ideal for hot climates.

In India, harem pants have been worn for centuries and were traditionally worn by both men and women. They were a common sight on the streets of India, especially in rural areas, where they were worn for work and other daily activities.

How to Style Baggy Indian Trousers

If you're new to wearing harem pants or dhoti pants, it can be challenging to know how to style them. Here are some tips to help you get started:

  • Pair them with a fitted top to balance out the loose fit of the pants.
  • Wear them with sandals or flats for a casual look, or dress them up with heels for a formal event.
  • Add a belt to define your waist and create a more structured look.
  • Accessorize with statement jewelry to add some extra flair to your outfit.

FAQs About Baggy Indian Trousers

What is the difference between harem pants and dhoti pants?

Harem pants are typically made from lightweight, flowy fabrics and taper at the ankles, while dhoti pants are made from a single piece of cloth and are wrapped around the legs like a loincloth.

Can men wear harem pants?

Yes, harem pants were traditionally worn by both men and women in the Middle East and India.

What is the best way to wash harem pants?

It's best to follow the care instructions on the label of your harem pants, but in general, they should be washed in cold water and hung to dry.

Where can I buy harem pants?

You can find harem pants at most Indian clothing stores, as well as online retailers like Amazon and Etsy.

Conclusion of What Are Baggy Indian Trousers Called

Baggy Indian trousers are a comfortable, versatile garment that has been worn for centuries in India and the Middle East. Whether you call them harem pants or dhoti pants, they're a great choice for anyone looking for a breezy, comfortable pant that can be dressed up or down for any occasion.
